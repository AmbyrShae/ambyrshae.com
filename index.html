BENEFITS ELIGIBILITY SYSTEM

Streamlining the eligibility process across Hawaiian public assistance programs in order to provide services more quickly.

Learn more at https://humanservices.hawaii.gov/bessd/

I incorporated scrum / agile methodologies in order to improve team productivity. I also led designs for Release 0.7 and worked closely with product owners and dev team in order to meet our deadlines. I used iterative design to incorporate changes to enhance the application. All designs were done using Adobe XD.

PORT HAWAII INFORMATION MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

Tracking and managing all vessels that travel to and from ports in Hawaii. This includes cargo, tariffs, billing and more.

I incorporated scrum / agile methodologies within my team at the start of the project in order to meet a 3 year deadline. In addition, I led FE development and UI / UX design, which included creating mock-ups using Adobe XD. I integrated UX in our software development process. This increased team velocity from 4 points to 8 points per person since development could proceed with client approved designs. For the FE, we used Vue.js and I paired it with View UI for its simplistic components.

PUBLIC ASSISTANCE INFORMATION SYSTEM

Providing a digital solution for applying to SNAP / TANF benefits in order to promptly receive assistance.

Learn more at https://pais-benefits.dhs.hawaii.gov/

Before the Covid-19 pandemic, Hawaii SNAP / TANF applications were done on paper. When offices shutdown, it created a bottleneck for those who needed assistance. I designed the online application. Within a day of the site going live, there were 1000 applications in progress. My main design principles were to help users easily navigate and complete their applications. This included single column layout which was responsive to mobile devices and using bold colors for call to action elements. Designs were done using Adobe XD.

FIELD VISION

Helping field engineers easily navigate daily processes during plant shutdowns such as managing shifts, reporting incidents and submitting reports online and offline.

I collaborated with a 100+ member team and worked closely with business analysts, QAs and UX designers on a daily basis. I programmed in Angular and Spring Boot to build out offline reports. This assured that field engineers could easily manage, review and send out reports even in areas where internet service was not available.

Designing the future of recycling scrap metals.

I worked with a California owned recycling company that was looking for a better way of managing the ticketing system for recycling scrap metals. I did the requirement gathering and designed the user workflow.

BRAGGING RIGHTS

Empowering women to speak up about their achievements in a safe space in order to build confidence to advocate for themselves.

Learn more at https://ambyrshae.github.io/bragging-rights/braggers

Bragging Rights was a little side project I did while I was working at GE Digital. There was a Women's Group that I was a part of and we met bi-weekly to discuss hurdles women face in tech. I wanted to expand my FE knowledge while also helping out the group and that's when our monthly "Bragging Rights" formed. We used the website to show a short bio of the speaker and a graph of highs and lows of their life and career. It allowed women a safe space to open up about obstacles they faced but also to express moments where they were proud of themselves.

GE GIRLS

Teaching girls the wonders of STEM by creating exciting activities related to software development.

Learn more at https://ge-girls.com/

I did the project management for the GE Girls 2017 summer camp held at Tulane University. The program itself was a week long but the preparation took months of planning. I coordinated with volunteers, school administration, teachers, students and parents in order to make the program a success. My main goal was to excite and retain interest of middle school girls in STEM. We created engaging activities related to robotics and coding, encouraged friendships through social games and confidence builders and celebrated their successes with an end-of-camp picnic that included all staff, students and parents.
